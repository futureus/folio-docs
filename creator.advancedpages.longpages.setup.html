Setting up a long page

To create a long page, select the page you want to extend in the Issue Editor window and edit that page. Once inside the page, tap the settings cog button, this will bring up the Page Settings window. Part way down the settings page you will see "Custom Size", simply set the new width and height of your page (eg, 768 x 2048 would be a double height iPad page) and tap "Save changes".

Your canvas will now be the new size. You can drag the page around to position your assets.

Stacked pages, long pages and scrolling

Normally, full-screen slideshows will allow the reader to swipe to the next page when they reach the end of the slideshow, but this functionality becomes disabled when you are using long pages. This also applies to any kind of horizontal scrolling within a page, such as wide pages.

Put simply, every FutureFolio issue scrolls between pages left to right. If you use stacks and/or long pages, your pages will also scroll up and down. If you also then use a wide page and/or a full-screen slideshow, the user will not be able to swipe to the next page.

This is obviously bad, so you have two choices:

  • Turn off stacks and/or make your page a normal size. This will restore normal scrolling behaviour.
  • Avoid using horizontal scrolling on your page, either by making your slideshow less than full screen (thus giving the user a tappable area where they can scroll), or by avoiding wide pages.

Having trouble navigating the canvas?

Simply change the zoom level to Fit page, this will zoom you out to display the entire canvas. Alternatively use the lock button to lock all the layers in place.

Lock and Zoom buttons

Lock Layers and the Zoom button. The padlock locks all layers in place enabling you to scroll around without accidentally moving objects. The Zoom button (tapped here), brings up a number of magnifications for easy navigation.
