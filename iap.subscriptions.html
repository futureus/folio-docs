Subscriptions

Subscriptions follow the same pricing tiers as single issue sales (including free). See "Pricing Tiers" for details on the pricing tiers. Subscriptions are by default auto-renewing, regardless of term. If a customer takes up a 30 day subscription, this amounts to a life-time commitment which will only terminate when they explicitly cancel. The number of days in the term is the number of days between renewals, not the length of the subscription.

Apple's subscription terms are all time-based, rather than issue-based. They are either 1-day, 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, 180 days or 365 days. A time-based subscription term can be a challenge to explain to the user, as a fixed time period can give a different number of issues depending on the start date.

Note that App revenue is usually boosted by being featured on one of Apple's App Store lists, such as Top Paid, Top Grossing etc. This is worth bearing in mind when considering pricing structure. A subscription model that renews more frequently will bump up the revenue more frequently, and may sustain an app better by keeping it in the Top Grossing list.

Changing the subscription price

Ensure that you never increase the cost of an existing subscription, as this will switch off the auto-renewals for those on this option.
