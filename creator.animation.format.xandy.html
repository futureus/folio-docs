Working with X and Y

The X and Y co-ordinates of an object combine to form its position, and respond poorly to being manipulated independently. For example, setting Y to 100 over 5 seconds and setting X to 100 over 1 second will not work as you intend: the two positions will clash.

If you simply must animate X and Y independently, the best thing you can do is animate the X value on one layer, place it inside a box, then animate the Y value on the box.

X and Y movements can be specific either as an absolute value, eg x="25", or as a relative value, eg x="x+25" or y="y-100". The former will make the object move to X co-ordinate 25, whereas the latter will add 25 pixels to wherever the layer is right now.
