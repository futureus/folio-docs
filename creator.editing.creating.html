Creating and naming layers

A single rendition is likely to have many layers, such as pictures and text. Each of these layers appear separately in the layers panel so that you can select and manipulate them individually.

To create a new layer, choose from one of the layer types:

  • Box - a rectangle-shaped container that is useful to group layers together, or to provide scrollable sub-areas on your page.
  • Story - a text box containing HTML and CSS.
  • Image - any picture type such as slideshows or 360s.
  • Movie - a standalone movie object with a thumbnail graphic.
  • Table - a sortable, grouped data listing.
  • Map - a drop-in Google Map that lets you highlight locations and draw routes.
  • Annotation - a picture with user-revealable captions.

When you create any of these, they will appear in the centre of your canvas sized at 256x256 and named (eg) "New image layer."

You can change the name of a layer by pressing and holding on it to bring up the layer settings. This allows you to rename the layer and also optionally assign it a colour for easier location.

You can show the editing panel for the layer by clicking on the cog icon at the right of the cell, or by double-clicking the selected layer.
