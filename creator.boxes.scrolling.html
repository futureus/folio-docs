Scrolling Box Contents

You can place layers inside a box which are larger than the box itself. When you do this, the child layers will be cut off at the box edges.

You may wish to allow users to scroll within the box so that they can view the content which is initially cut off. To achieve this, click on the Layer Settings (cog) button for the box layer and then choose the required option on the Scroll to fit contents control:

  • Never - the user cannot scroll in either direction. Contents remain cut off.
  • Vertical - the user may scroll vertically, but any horizontal overspill is cut off.
  • Horizontal - the user may scroll horizontally, but any vertical overspill is cut off.
  • Both - the user may scroll vertically and horizontally.

Boxes can be set to paged scrolling both with (Paging dots option) or without paging dots (On option). Simply select the desired scrolling type under the Scroll in pages option. Paging will honour vertical and horizontal scrolling and will snap to the size of your box for each page unit.

Paging dots will only honour the Horizontal scrolling setting in Scroll to fit contents, vertical pages will not show page dotting regardless of your settings.

Box scrolling to the edge

Not that when a user scrolls horizontally within a box layer, if they reach the edge of the content and keep scrolling, the page will pick up the scroll movement. This just means that if they keep going, they may eventually scroll to the next page in the issue.
